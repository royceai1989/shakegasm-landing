// Voice

How ShakeGasm writes, and what it never does.

Rule

Lead with the answer

The payoff goes first, the setup never gets a turn. "Game night just got loud," not "Have you ever wondered how to liven up game night?"

Rule

Every sentence carries a fact

A number, a venue, a mechanic. Sixty seconds. One phone. Six rivals. Beast mode. Concrete beats vague every time.

Rule

Cheeky, never adult

Confident and a little smug, like the friend who turns any room into a competition. Suggestive is off the table. This is a party game, SFW everywhere.

Rule

No em-dashes, no en-dashes

Commas and full stops only. No long dashes of any kind in copy. The engine rejects any post that contains one.

Rule

Name the occasion

Pre-drinks, road trip, the queue, date night, the family table. Specific moments out-pull generic "fun for everyone".

Rule

Kill the filler

No moreover, furthermore, unlock, leverage, utilize, various, myriad, actually, really, very, just. If a word is throat-clearing, cut it.

Rule

Stakes over features

Loser picks the restaurant beats "supports two players". Give people something to win or lose.

// Illustrations

The S and the shake are the language.

Flat vector, one accent, pink to purple at most. The S mark, motion lines, the meter, the badge. No 3D, no stock packs.

Do
  • Flat vector, rounded caps, even stroke
  • Pink to purple gradient or one solid hot pink
  • Motion lines, meters, badges, the S
Don’t
  • 3D isometric or glossy renders
  • Character illustrations or mascots
  • Stock vector packs and clip-art
