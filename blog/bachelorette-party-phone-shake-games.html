A bachelorette session works best in three blocks of 30 minutes. Block one runs warmup rounds: two solo shakes and one duet, total 18 minutes including walk-up time. Block three runs the final and the bride round, where the bride faces the leaderboard top three back to back with her score weighted at 1.5x. Total active gameplay clocks in at 47 minutes. Five rules a host should set before round one

Hosts who skipped a pre-round briefing reported a 40 percent drop in finish-the-game rates across our pilot weekends. Five rules, read aloud in 60 seconds, fixed it.

  • Phones face up on the table between rounds. Nobody pre-shakes to inflate scores.
  • One shake hand only. Two-hand grips win every time and flatten the bracket.
  • A 3-second countdown from the host, no early starts. Late starts cost 200 points.
  • Bride scores carry a 1.5x bonus in the final block, applied by the scoreboard.
  • Phone-handoff rounds use the bride's phone, so haptic feedback stays consistent.

The rules sound strict on paper, but the bracket format rewards anyone who reads them once. Hosts who used the script verbatim ran a 90-minute session without a single dispute across 11 pilot nights.

Planning mistakes that flatten a bachelorette session

The pilot weekends surfaced five repeat failures, each one fixable in under two minutes of prep work.
